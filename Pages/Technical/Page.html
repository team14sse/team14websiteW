Genetic Algorithm

Inspired by Evolution

Based on The nature of code

Introduction

Genetic Algorithm tries to simulate the evolution of the animal species.

The simple process of evolution starts with a population of animals. Each of them have genes that define their characteristics and fitness. The fittest animals have the highest chance of surviving due to their superior genes and therefore have a higher chance of mating, passing down said genes. Genes and animals are the same concepts in this context.

The offspring’s genes’ are composed of some part of father’s genes and some part of mother’s genes. However, occasionally genes may mutate, producing different characteristics that creates a variance among the population. Eventually, all of the original animals in the population will be replaced by the offspring, thus creating a new generation. This process is repeated many times, and hopefully, the overall fitness of a population increases.

The ultimate goal of this algorithm is to optimize (find the best value for) the fitness function.

Similarities between Genetic Algorithm to a simple evolution process.

As you can see in the figure above, the genetic algorithm starts with an initializing process: the creation of the first generation. It is initialized through a random process and the evaluation of the fitness of the first generation will be part of a selection process.

  • During selection, we will pick an animal with a high fitness (not the highest), using a process called a tournament selection. This starts with a sample in which we randomly select an animal/gene n times where n is defined as a tournament round. After sampling n times, we pick the gene that has the highest fitness among the group.
  • In the crossover process, we will get 2 animals that pass the selection process to mate, which will output the gene of the offspring.
  • In the mutation process, we will roll a dice which will indicate where and how we change the gene.

The process will repeat until we get the new generation. We can create as many generations as we want and hope that the fitness of the younger generation is higher than the older.

Why do we use Genetic Algorithm? (Implementation)

Infinite monkey theorem.

This theorem states that given enough time, and enough monkeys typing on keyboards, eventually they will finish the complete stories of William Shakespeare. As you can imagine, there is a small probability that it works. Our goal is to use the genetic algorithm to help these monkeys finish their job in our lifetime.

Mutatation

The process is simple, for each of the characters in a gene we toss a biased coin that has 99% chance of being heads (won’t change the gene) and 1% chance of being a tail (replace the current one with a random character).

In the code we start by defining a list “a”, then we loop through a gene and if the rate (which will be 0.01) is more the random.random() (finds a random float between 0 to 1) we will add a random character to a list, else we just add the original. Then we set genes to be the mutated one.

This fitness function is quite simple. We start with the target sentence. We then increase the fitness by one of the characters until the gene matches the target sentence.

We start by setting fitness to zero. Then we compare the gene and target sentence. After that, we store the fitness as a class variable.
