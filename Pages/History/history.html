History of Software Systems Engineering

A Brief Introduction

Early 1940s

The term Systems Engineering first appeared in Bell Telephone Laboratories.

1946

The RAND Corporation was founded by the United States Air Force and created systems analysis, which is an important part of systems engineering.

Late 1940s

The Department of Defense entered the world of systems engineering with the initial development of missiles and missile-defense systems.
Paul Fitts addressed the allocation of the systems functions to the physical elements of the system.

1950

The first attempt to teach systems engineering at MIT by Mr. Gilman, Director of Systems Engineering at Bell.

Late 1960s

Standards for System Engineering have emerged first appear in military standards.

1990

The National Council on Systems Engineering (NCOSE) was founded.

NCOSE was created to address the need to improve systems engineering practices and education.

1995

SP-6105 was originally released to bring the basic concepts and techniques of systems engineering to the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) personnel to identify the nature of NASA systems and the NASA environment.
Since then, systems engineering at NASA, within national and international standard bodies, and as a discipline has undergone rapid evolution.

1999

The Software Systems Engineering Group, a constituent part of the University College London, Department of Computer Science was formally set up.
